Concept

Problem Statement & Market Opportunity

"Indian women are satisfied on dating apps but not delighted."
This gap presents a significant opportunity in India, where dating apps suffer from a highly skewed gender ratio of 80:20 (men to women). Our guiding principle: if we can attract women to our platform, men will follow.

Key Problems Identified

Through our research with:

  • 30+ female dating app user Inteviews
  • Product managers from Bumble and Tinder
  • One month of market study

Two major issues emerged:

  • Safety within the app: Challenges include sexual messages, stalking, and other forms of harassment.
  • Quality of men's profiles ("good crowd"):

Solution Approach

Safety: We aim to address this using AI and other technological solutions.
Improving Profile Quality:

  • Misconception: Higher subscription prices lead to better profiles.
  • Actual Solution: A rigorous onboarding process, similar to Hinge's.
  • Key Insight: Strict onboarding for men, with a more relaxed process for women, as profile quality is primarily a female-specific demand.

In short, we will to build a platform better than Hinge, leveraging our lower capital requirements to keep prices affordable. Our basic plan will be free for women to encourage onboarding.

Market Analysis

Total Addressable Market (TAM)

Our TAM includes all global dating app users.
We see this as an interaction problem, not a dating problem, and intend to address this gap by entering the market with a targeted approach before diversifying through multiple apps. Effective messaging is key to resonating with our target audience; thus, one app cannot satisfy the entire world.

Revenue Calculations

Subscription Revenue

Male Users:

  • Total male users: 8 lakh
  • Paying users (20%): 1.6 lakh
  • Average monthly revenue per paying user:
    • Premium (₹99/6): ₹16.50 (Assumes user buys monthly subscriptions twice a year)
    • Roses (3 × ₹20): ₹60
    • Spotlight (1 × ₹25): ₹25
    • Comments (1 × ₹25): ₹25
    • Total monthly ARPU: ₹126.50
  • Annual revenue: 1.6 lakh × ₹126.50 × 12 = ₹2.436 crore

Female Users:

  • Total female users: 10 lakh
  • Paying users (10%): 1 lakh
  • Average monthly revenue per paying user:
    • Advanced filter (₹40/6): ₹6.67 (Assumes user buys monthly subscriptions twice a year)
    • Roses (2 × ₹20): ₹40
    • Comments (1 × ₹25): ₹25
    • Total monthly ARPU: ₹71.67
  • Annual revenue: 1 lakh × ₹71.67 × 12 = ₹8.6 crore

Advertising Revenue

  • Total users: 18 lakh
  • Daily ads seen per user: 2 (We assume that each user gets 15 free likes per day and thus will swipe an average of 30 times per day—15 likes and 15 dislikes)
  • Annual impressions: 18 lakh × 2 × 365 = 131.4 crore
  • Average ad rate: ₹0.75 per impression
  • Annual ad revenue: 131.4 crore × ₹0.75 = ₹98.5 crore

Total Annual Revenue

  • Subscription revenue: ₹24.336 crore + ₹8.6004 crore = ₹32.9364 crore
  • Advertising revenue: ₹98.55 crore
  • Total annual revenue: ₹131.4 crore
